---> Current Situation: 
-Parent: fixed inset-0 bg-black (no flex).
-Video: w-full h-full (but without object-cover).

Even without flex items-center justify-center,
the video still looks centered. Here's why it's still "centered":

-- w-full h-full makes the video try to fill the entire parent (which is full screen because of fixed inset-0).
By default, browsers treat <video> elements with aspect ratio preservation.
-- When you use w-full h-full without object-cover, the video behaves similarly to object-contain.
It scales to fit inside the container while keeping its original proportions.
-- This creates black bars (letterboxing) on the sides or top/bottom, depending on the video’s aspect ratio vs screen ratio.
-- Because of this, the video appears visually centered inside the full-screen black container.
